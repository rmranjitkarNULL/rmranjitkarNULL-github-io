4-DoF Serial Manipulator: Pick and Place

The 4-DoF Serial Manipulator project demonstrates a complete vision-guided robotic pick-and-place system developed in MATLAB. It integrates forward and inverse kinematics, trajectory generation, and computer vision to autonomously detect, grasp, and sort colored objects. Using a calibrated camera and a Dynamixel-based arm, the robot achieved sub-millimeter localization accuracy and smooth, repeatable motion control.

System Architecture

The manipulator integrates two main subsystems: a vision system for detecting and localizing objects, and a robot control system for motion execution. A calibrated USB camera captures the workspace and provides object coordinates to the robot controller. Modular MATLAB classes (ImageProcessor and Robot) manage perception and motion independently, ensuring scalability and robustness.

Kinematic Modeling & Motion Control

Motion was governed by both analytical and numerical inverse kinematics. Forward kinematics were derived using Denavitâ€“Hartenberg parameters, while analytical inverse kinematics provided efficient closed-form joint solutions. A Damped Least Squares (DLS) numerical solver complemented this approach for stability near singularities. Smooth trajectories were generated using cubic and quintic interpolation, enabling continuous and precise motion.

Computer Vision & Calibration

The computer vision subsystem used intrinsic and extrinsic calibration to map camera pixels to real-world robot coordinates. A color-segmentation pipeline isolated colored objects on a checkerboard surface, computed centroids, and transformed them into 3-D task-space coordinates. This mapping enabled autonomous pick-and-place execution with a mean localization error of ~1 mm.

Results and Performance

The system achieved reliable and precise vision-guided manipulation. Intrinsic calibration error was below 1 pixel, and grasp success exceeded 94% across 30 trials. Real-world positional error averaged 1 mm, and motion trajectories showed smooth cubic interpolation between key poses. Each pick-and-place cycle completed in ~7 seconds.
